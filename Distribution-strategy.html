Project Overview

Nairobi Refreshments Ltd, a leading beverage manufacturer in Kenya, approached Fadan Consultant Limited with a critical challenge: despite having quality products and strong brand recognition, they were experiencing suboptimal sales performance across retail locations in Nairobi.

Initial analysis revealed that poor merchandising practices were significantly impacting product visibility and consumer purchasing decisions. The client's beverages were often poorly positioned, inconsistently stocked, and lacked strategic shelf placement across different retail formats.

Fadan was engaged to develop and implement a comprehensive merchandising optimization strategy that would enhance product visibility, improve brand presence, and ultimately drive sales growth across more than 200 retail locations in Nairobi.

Project Objectives

  • Increase product visibility across all retail channels
  • Develop standardized merchandising guidelines
  • Optimize shelf space allocation and product placement
  • Implement effective point-of-sale materials
  • Train merchandising staff on best practices

Key Metrics

  • Product visibility score across retail locations
  • Share of shelf space compared to competitors
  • Merchandising compliance rate
  • Sales performance by retail channel
  • Return on merchandising investment

The Challenge

Through comprehensive market analysis and retail audits, we identified several critical challenges affecting Nairobi Refreshments' merchandising effectiveness:

Inconsistent Implementation

Merchandising standards varied significantly across different retail locations, creating an inconsistent brand experience for consumers.

Poor Shelf Positioning

Products were frequently placed in low-visibility areas, reducing the likelihood of consumer engagement and purchase.

Inadequate Staff Training

Merchandising personnel lacked proper training on product placement, stock rotation, and display maintenance.

Ineffective POS Materials

Point-of-sale materials were outdated, damaged, or inconsistently deployed across retail locations.

Additionally, the client faced significant competition for shelf space from both multinational and local beverage brands. With limited retail real estate available, securing optimal positioning was a constant challenge.

The fragmented retail landscape in Nairobi, consisting of modern trade supermarkets, small format stores, and traditional dukas (small shops), further complicated the implementation of a standardized merchandising approach.

Our Approach

Fadan developed a comprehensive merchandising optimization strategy based on data-driven insights and industry best practices. Our approach focused on five key pillars:

1

Retail Audit & Analysis

We conducted comprehensive audits across 200+ retail locations to assess current merchandising practices, shelf space allocation, and competitor positioning.

Our team collected data on product visibility, share of shelf, display quality, and compliance with existing merchandising guidelines to establish a baseline for improvement.

2

Merchandising Strategy Development

Based on audit findings, we developed a tailored merchandising strategy that addressed specific challenges across different retail formats.

The strategy included planograms for optimal product placement, category management principles, and visual merchandising techniques to enhance brand presence.

3

POS Material Redesign

We redesigned point-of-sale materials to create a cohesive brand experience across all retail touchpoints.

New materials included shelf talkers, wobblers, aisle violators, and promotional displays that effectively communicated brand messaging and product benefits.

4

Merchandising Team Training

We developed and implemented a comprehensive training program for the client's merchandising team, focusing on best practices, planogram implementation, and performance monitoring.

Training sessions included both classroom instruction and in-store practical applications to ensure consistent implementation.

5

Monitoring & Optimization System

We implemented a digital monitoring system that allowed for real-time tracking of merchandising compliance and performance across all retail locations.

The system included a mobile app for field teams to document implementation, report issues, and receive immediate feedback on merchandising quality.

Strategic Considerations

Our approach was tailored to address the unique challenges of the Kenyan retail landscape, including:

  • Varying store formats and sizes, from large supermarkets to small dukas
  • Different shopper behaviors across socioeconomic segments
  • Seasonal variations in beverage consumption patterns
  • Local competitive dynamics and retailer relationships

Implementation Process

The implementation phase was structured as a systematic rollout across all 200+ retail locations, with careful monitoring and adjustment throughout the process.

1

Pilot Implementation

Initial rollout in 20 key stores to test and refine the merchandising strategy before full-scale implementation.

2

Phased Rollout

Systematic implementation across remaining stores, prioritized by sales volume and strategic importance.

3

Continuous Optimization

Ongoing monitoring and refinement based on performance data and field team feedback.

Key Implementation Components

1. Planogram Implementation

We developed custom planograms for each store format, ensuring optimal product placement based on eye-level positioning, category flow, and brand blocking principles.

Implementation teams worked directly with store staff to reset shelves according to the new planograms, ensuring proper execution and buy-in from retail partners.

2. Visual Merchandising Enhancement
